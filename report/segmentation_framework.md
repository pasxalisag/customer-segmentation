# Customer Segmentation Framework

## Segmentation Logic
Customers are segmented based on a combination of revenue contribution, purchase frequency, and relationship maturity.  
The objective is to group pharmacy accounts into meaningful segments that support sales prioritization and account management decisions.

---

## Key Segmentation Metrics
- **Total Revenue**: Overall customer value
- **Total Invoices**: Purchase frequency
- **Average Order Value (AOV)**: Order quality
- **Months Active**: Relationship duration

---

## Defined Customer Segments

### 1. Strategic Accounts
**Profile**
- High revenue contribution
- High purchase frequency
- Long-term active relationship

**Business Role**
- Core revenue drivers
- Require proactive relationship management

**Recommended Actions**
- Priority account management
- Customized commercial offers
- Cross-selling and upselling initiatives

---

### 2. Growth Accounts
**Profile**
- Medium revenue
- Growing purchase frequency
- Moderate relationship duration

**Business Role**
- High growth potential
- Suitable for development initiatives

**Recommended Actions**
- Targeted promotions
- Product portfolio expansion
- Increased sales engagement

---

### 3. Stable Accounts
**Profile**
- Consistent but moderate revenue
- Regular purchasing behavior
- Stable relationship duration

**Business Role**
- Reliable revenue contributors
- Lower management intensity

**Recommended Actions**
- Maintain current engagement
- Monitor for growth or decline signals

---

### 4. At-Risk Accounts
**Profile**
- Low revenue
- Low purchase frequency
- Short or declining relationship duration

**Business Role**
- Risk of churn
- Reactivation opportunities

**Recommended Actions**
- Reactivation campaigns
- Targeted offers
- Root-cause analysis for decline
